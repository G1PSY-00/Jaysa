Human Evolution

Human evolution is the long process where humans developed from ancient, ape-like ancestors over millions of years, marked by key changes like bipedalism (walking on two legs), larger brains, tool use, and complex language, leading to modern Homo sapiens as the sole surviving human species today. This journey involved many extinct human relatives, such as Australopithecines and Neanderthals, and is driven by natural selection and adaptation to changing environments
