The Universe

The universe began around 13.8 billion years ago with the Big Bang. It contains stars, galaxies, planets, black holes, and dark matter that influence its structure. Cosmic events like supernovae and collisions continuously shape the universe. Studying these phenomena helps us understand how the cosmos evolves over time.

Solar System

Our solar system has eight planets, countless moons, asteroids, and comets, each with unique characteristics. Space missions like Mars rovers and Voyager probes have revealed new details about these celestial bodies. Understanding our solar system helps us prepare for future exploration beyond Earth.

Human Evolution

Humans evolved from early primates to modern Homo sapiens. Key milestones include walking upright, using tools, and developing complex brains and cultures. Studying human evolution provides insight into our origins and how we might adapt for life in space.
